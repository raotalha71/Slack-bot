EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Hartwell Logistics operates six distribution hubs across the Midwest with approximately 200 staff. Over the past three years, rapid growth has outpaced the company's internal tooling. Inventory data lives in disconnected systems at each hub, reporting is manual and time-consuming, and operational decisions are being made without real-time visibility across the network.

This proposal outlines a phased approach to building a unified supply chain visibility platform that connects all six hubs, automates routine reporting, and provides leadership with a real-time operational dashboard. The solution is designed to integrate with Hartwell's existing ERP (SAP Business One) and avoid a full system replacement.

---

PROPOSED SOLUTION

Phase 1 — Data Integration Layer (Months 1-2)
Build a lightweight integration layer that pulls inventory data from each hub's existing system into a central database. No hub systems are replaced. Data syncs every 15 minutes. A simple internal dashboard shows consolidated inventory across all locations.

Phase 2 — Reporting Automation (Month 3)
Replace the current manual Monday reporting process with automated weekly and daily ops reports generated from the central database. Reports delivered via email and accessible through the dashboard.

Phase 3 — Alerting and Workflow (Months 4-5)
Add low-stock alerts, cross-hub transfer recommendations, and a simple approval workflow for inter-hub inventory moves.

---

TIMELINE

Month 1-2: Integration layer and central database
Month 3: Automated reporting
Month 4-5: Alerting, workflows, and staff training
Month 6: Stabilisation and handover
